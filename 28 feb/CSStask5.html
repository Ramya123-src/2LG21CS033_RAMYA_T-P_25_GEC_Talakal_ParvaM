Bora Bora

Island in French

Bora Bora, also known as Porapora, is a volcanic island in French Polynesia, located in the central South Pacific Ocean, about 165 miles northwest of Tahiti

Bora-Bora is a volcanic island in the central South Pacific Ocean. It is part of the Leeward Islands, in the Society Islands of French Polynesia. Bora-Bora lies about 165 miles (265 kilometers) northwest of Tahiti. Bora-Bora is spelled Porapora in Tahitian and means “first born.” According to tradition, it was the first island created after Raiatea, the largest of the Leeward Islands.

India Gate

Historical Monument

India Gate, originally called the All India War Memorial, is a monument in New Delhi that commemorates 74,187 Indian Army soldiers who died between 1914 and 1921 during World War I, in various locations including France, Flanders, Mesopotamia, Persia, East Africa, Gallipoli, and the Third Anglo-Afghan War

At the centre of New Delhi stands the 42 m high India Gate, an “Arc-de-Triomphe” like archway in the middle of a crossroad. Almost similar to its French counterpart, it commemorates the 70,000 Indian soldiers who lost their lives fighting for the British Army during the World War I..
